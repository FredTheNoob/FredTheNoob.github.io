Becoming friends with your camera

Part 1: Selfie: The Wrong Way vs. The Right Way

Picture 1: The wrong way (No zoom)

Picture 2: The right way (Zoomed in)

Comparing Picture 1 and Picture 2, we see less distortion in Picture 2. The distortion makes the nose, chin and forehead of the subject look disproportionately large. As we get further away and zoom in, the relative size differences between the near and far features shrink producing a flat look. Furthermore, the composition of the image is improved, as we zoom in, we remove more of the background putting the subject in the center.

Part 2: Architectural Perspective Compression

Picture 1: Further away - Zoomed in

Picture 2: Up close - No zoom

By default, phone cameras have a wide angle lens, that way we can capture more depth. The first tree on Picture 2 looks much larger than the trees in the background, compared to Picture 1, where all trees seems to have more or less the same size, creating a more compressed look due to the long focal length.
